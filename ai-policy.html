The short version

Marloo uses AI to help you work faster. Your client data is never used to train AI models. The AI processes your data in real time and discards it immediately. You remain in control.

Which AI models does Marloo use?

Marloo uses two AI providers to power its features:

Provider What it does Data retained?
Anthropic (Claude) Meeting summaries, document analysis, advice notes No — zero data retention
OpenAI (GPT) Supporting AI features No — zero data retention

Both providers operate under Zero Data Retention (ZDR) enterprise agreements with Marloo. This means:

  • Your data is sent to the model to generate a response
  • The moment the response is returned, the data is discarded
  • It is never stored, logged, or used for training

Will AI models train on my client data?

No. This is the most important question financial advisers ask us, and the answer is unambiguous.

Anthropic and OpenAI are contractually prohibited from using any data processed through Marloo's enterprise API to train their models. This applies to:

  • Meeting transcripts
  • Client documents you upload
  • Advice notes and summaries
  • Any other content processed through Marloo

The ZDR agreements are a core part of our enterprise contracts with both providers — not an optional setting.

Who transcribes my meetings?

Meeting recordings are transcribed using AssemblyAI. Transcription data is processed in real time and is not retained by AssemblyAI beyond the immediate processing window.

Meeting bots that join your calls are powered by Recall. Recall captures the audio/video stream for the purpose of transcription only.

Both AssemblyAI and Recall are bound by data processing agreements with Marloo and do not retain or use your data for any purpose beyond transcription.

Where does AI processing happen?

AI inference (the moment your data is sent to a model and a response is generated) takes place on servers operated by Anthropic and OpenAI, which are based in the United States.

This constitutes an international data transfer under UK GDPR. Marloo manages this lawfully through Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with both providers, supplemented by the UK Extension to the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework.

What data is sent to AI models?

Only the data needed to generate the output you've requested is sent. For example:

  • When generating a meeting summary → the transcript of that meeting
  • When analysing a document → the content of that document
  • When drafting an advice note → the relevant meeting and client context you provide

Marloo does not send data to AI models in the background or for purposes beyond generating your requested output.

Your obligations as a data controller

Under UK GDPR, your firm is the data controller for your clients' personal data. Marloo acts as your data processor.

This means:

  • You are responsible for ensuring you have a lawful basis to share client data with Marloo (typically legitimate interests or client consent)
  • You should inform clients that their meeting data may be processed by an AI tool
  • Signing Marloo's Data Processing Agreement (DPA) satisfies your Article 28 UK GDPR obligation for a controller-to-processor agreement

If you need support with client disclosure language, contact compliance@gomarloo.com.
